# Optimizing-Lifecycle-Retention-for-SaaS
For any subscription-based business, revenue isn't just about the first sale, it’s about the "Attach Rate" and "Retention." I took on the ONYX Data Challenge to analyze 2024–2025 performance data, specifically looking for the "leakage" in the sales funnel where customers were dropping off before their second billing cycle.

I developed a 5-page end-to-end commercial suite to analyze the sales ecosystem of a global software retailer. By mapping the "Golden Path" from first purchase to second transaction, I uncovered the specific channels and discount strategies that drive long-term retention and maximized Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).

# The Solution

Multi-Dimensional Modeling: Built a comprehensive reporting suite in Power BI that tracks the entire customer journey, from initial acquisition channel to refund analysis.

Cohort & Retention Analysis: Engineered metrics to calculate the "Time to Second Purchase" (Average vs. Median), allowing the business to pinpoint the exact window when a customer is most likely to churn.

# Dashboard Details
This report explores sales performance for a global software retailer that sells subscriptions and add-ons across analytics, design, collaboration, and AI. Using the data, it identifies loyal customers and understands which channels, products, and promotions encourage repeat purchases while revealing the main drivers of overall revenue.

# Page 1 – Overall Revenue Performance
This page provides a broad view of how sales evolve across months and channels. It includes key indicators such as total revenue, units sold, and how performance changes over 2024–2025. The visuals also show which sales channels consistently generate the most revenue, helping clarify where the strongest growth comes from.

# Page 2 – Loyal Customer Insights
Here the focus is on repeat buyers. This section highlights how much revenue loyal customers contribute, which channels bring in the most repeat purchases, and how loyalty trends shift month to month. It also shows the products most commonly bought by returning customers, making it easier to understand what keeps them coming back.

# Page 3 – Product and Discount Performance
This part examines how individual products and discount codes shape purchasing behavior. It identifies the highest-earning products, shows how often different discount codes are used, and reveals which of those codes actually lead to repeat purchases. It also includes the attach rate for add-ons, offering a clearer picture of how well core products drive additional sales.

# Page 4 – Pricing and Refund Analysis
This page looks at how pricing varies across markets and where refund issues are most common. It includes average selling prices by country and currency, along with a breakdown of refunds by channel and product. These visuals help pinpoint pricing variations and highlight where customer experience challenges may be appearing.

# Page 5 – Retention and Relationship Metrics
The final page examines customer behavior after their first purchase. It shows the average and median time to a second purchase, revenue per customer across billing cycles, and the overall time customers take between their first and second transactions. This section helps explain retention patterns and the effectiveness of different plan types.

In all the five pages, the report shows how customers buy, what drives them to return, and which products, channels, and promotions support long-term revenue and loyalty.
